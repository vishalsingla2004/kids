1. Speed, Distance, Time
Formula Triangle
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
D = S × T  |  T = D ÷ S
Cover the quantity you want to find in the triangle — the remaining two show the calculation. Cover D: see S × T. Cover S: see D ÷ T.
Worked Example
Car travels 150 km in 3 hours
Speed = 150 ÷ 3 = 50 km/h
2. Force and Newton's Laws
Newton's 2nd Law
F = m × a
(Force = mass × acceleration)
1st Law (Inertia)
Objects stay still or keep moving at the same speed unless a force acts on them.
3rd Law (Action-Reaction)
Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.
Worked Example
Mass = 5 kg, acceleration = 3 m/s²
F = 5 × 3 = 15 N (Newtons)
3. Mass vs Weight
Weight Formula
W = m × g
g = 10 N/kg on Earth · 1.6 on Moon
  • Mass (kg) — amount of matter, same everywhere
  • Weight (N) — gravitational pull, varies by location
  • Mass is measured with a balance; weight with a scale
Example
Person, mass = 60 kg
Weight on Earth = 60 × 10 = 600 N
Weight on Moon = 60 × 1.6 = 96 N
4. Energy Types
Energy Formulas
KE = ½mv²   GPE = mgh
Energy cannot be created or destroyed — only transformed. At the top of the hill all energy is GPE. At the bottom it becomes KE. Total stays the same (conservation of energy).
  • KE = kinetic energy (moving objects)
  • GPE = gravitational potential energy (height)
  • Other types: heat, sound, light, electrical, nuclear
5. Electricity Basics
Ohm's Law
V = I × R
Voltage (V) = Current (A) × Resistance (Ω)
  • Voltage (V) — electrical pressure (Volts)
  • Current (I) — flow of electrons (Amps)
  • Resistance (R) — opposition to flow (Ohms, Ω)
Example
A torch runs on V = 3V with R = 6 Ω
Current I = V ÷ R = 3 ÷ 6 = 0.5 A
6. Magnetism
Key Rules
N–S attract · N–N repel
Every magnet has a North and South pole. Field lines go from North to South outside the magnet. You can never separate the two poles — cut a magnet in half and you get two smaller magnets.
  • Earth is a giant magnet (geographic north = magnetic south)
  • Magnetic fields can pass through most materials
  • Electromagnets: electric current creates magnetism
🟡 Medium
1. Ohm's Law
Three Forms
V = I × R
I = V ÷ R    R = V ÷ I
Use the triangle to find any quantity. Cover what you want — the rest shows the calculation. Units: Voltage in Volts (V), Current in Amps (A), Resistance in Ohms (Ω).
Worked Example
V = 12 V, R = 4 Ω
I = 12 ÷ 4 = 3 A
2. Series vs Parallel Circuits
Resistance Rules
Series: R = R1 + R2
Parallel: 1/R = 1/R1 + 1/R2
  • Series: break one bulb → all go out
  • Parallel: break one bulb → rest stay on (house wiring!)
  • Parallel gives smaller total resistance
3. Work and Power
Formulas
Work = F × d (Joules, J)
Power = Work ÷ Time (Watts, W)
Work is done when a force moves an object. Power is how quickly work is done. A Watt = 1 Joule per second.
Worked Example
Lift a 50 N box a distance of 3 m in 5 seconds
Work = 50 × 3 = 150 J
Power = 150 ÷ 5 = 30 W
4. Waves — Frequency and Wavelength
Wave Speed Formula
v = f × λ
speed = frequency (Hz) × wavelength (m)
  • Amplitude — height of wave (energy)
  • Wavelength (λ) — length of one full wave
  • Frequency (f) — waves per second (Hz)
  • Light speed: 3×10⁸ m/s · Sound in air: ~340 m/s
5. Pressure
Formula
P = F ÷ A
Pressure (Pa) = Force (N) ÷ Area (m²)
Same force but smaller area → much higher pressure. That's why a sharp needle pierces skin easily but a thumb doesn't. 1 Pascal (Pa) = 1 N/m²
Worked Example
Force = 100 N, area = 0.5 m²
P = 100 ÷ 0.5 = 200 Pa
6. Density
Formula
ρ = m ÷ V
density = mass ÷ volume (kg/m³ or g/cm³)
  • Water density = 1 g/cm³
  • Density > water → sinks
  • Density < water → floats
  • Iron ≈ 7.87 g/cm³ · Wood ≈ 0.5 g/cm³
Example
Mass = 200 g, Volume = 40 cm³
ρ = 200 ÷ 40 = 5 g/cm³ → sinks in water
🔴 Hard
1. Acceleration and Motion Graphs
SUVAT Equations
v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
  • Slope of d-t graph = speed
  • Slope of v-t graph = acceleration
  • Area under v-t graph = distance travelled
  • s=displacement, u=initial v, v=final v, a=accel, t=time
2. Gravitational PE and Kinetic Energy
Energy Equations
GPE = mgh   KE = ½mv²
At the top: maximum GPE, minimum KE. At the bottom: maximum KE, minimum GPE. Total mechanical energy is conserved (ignoring friction): GPE + KE = constant.
Example
Mass = 800 kg, height = 20 m
GPE at top = 800×10×20 = 160,000 J
KE at bottom = 160,000 J → v = √(2×160,000÷800) ≈ 20 m/s
3. AC vs DC Electricity
Key Difference
DC — constant direction (battery)
AC — alternates direction (mains)
  • UK mains: 230 V AC at 50 Hz
  • AC reverses direction 100 times per second (2×50 Hz)
  • Transformers only work with AC — used to step voltage up/down
  • DC used in all electronics, phones, computers
4. Refraction of Light
Snell's Law (simplified)
n = sin(θᵢ) ÷ sin(θᵣ)
Light bends toward the normal when entering a denser medium (e.g. air → glass). It bends away from the normal when leaving (glass → air). Total internal reflection occurs when the angle exceeds the critical angle.
  • Glass refractive index n ≈ 1.5
  • Critical angle for glass ≈ 42°
  • Used in: optical fibres, lenses, diamonds
5. Nuclear Structure
Key Numbers
Atomic number = protons
Mass number = protons + neutrons
  • Electrons orbit the nucleus in shells
  • Isotopes: same protons, different neutrons
  • Alpha (α): least penetrating, stopped by paper
  • Beta (β): stopped by aluminium
  • Gamma (γ): most penetrating, needs lead/concrete
6. Electromagnetic Spectrum
Order (low → high frequency)
Radio → Micro → IR → Visible → UV → X-ray → Gamma
  • All EM waves travel at 3×10⁸ m/s in a vacuum
  • Higher frequency = shorter wavelength = more energy
  • Radio: TV/radio signals · Micro: cooking/Wi-Fi
  • IR: heat sensing · UV: causes sunburn
  • X-ray: medical imaging · Gamma: cancer treatment
