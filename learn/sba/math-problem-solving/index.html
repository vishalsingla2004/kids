1. Read the Problem
Problem Strategy
CUBES method
Circle numbers · Underline question · Box key words
Before solving, identify what information is given and what you need to find. Circle all numbers, underline the question, and box key operation words like "total," "left," or "each."
Worked Example
Given: 3/4 cup, uses 1/4 cup
Find: how much remains
Solve: 3/4 − 1/4 = 2/4 = 1/2 cup
2. Money Math
Money Formula
Change = Paid − Total Cost
Line up decimal points when adding prices. Subtract total from amount paid to find change. Always include the dollar sign and two decimal places in your answer.
Worked Example
Buy $2.35 + $1.50, pay $5.00
Total = $3.85
Change = $5.00 − $3.85 = $1.15
3. Fraction of a Quantity
Fraction of a Whole
fraction × total = part
To find a fraction of a quantity, multiply the fraction by the total. The numerator tells you how many parts you want; the denominator tells you how many equal parts the whole is split into.
Worked Example
3/4 of 24 students:
3/4 × 24 = 72/4 = 18 students
4. Area in Context
Area Formula
Area = length × width
Area measures the space inside a flat shape. For rectangles, multiply length by width. Label your answer in square units (sq ft, sq cm, etc.).
Worked Example
Room 12 ft × 9 ft:
A = 12 × 9 = 108 sq ft
5. Perimeter in Context
Perimeter Formula
P = 2l + 2w = 2(l + w)
Perimeter is the total distance around the outside of a shape. Add all side lengths. For rectangles, the two lengths are equal and the two widths are equal, so use 2l + 2w.
Worked Example
Garden 8 m × 5 m:
P = 2(8 + 5) = 2 × 13 = 26 m
6. Basic Volume
Volume Formula
V = l × w × h
Volume measures space inside a 3D box. Multiply all three dimensions together. The answer uses cubic units (cm³, in³, ft³). Label units correctly!
Worked Example
Fish tank 20 × 10 × 12 cm:
V = 20 × 10 × 12 = 2,400 cm³
🟡 Medium
7. Multi-Step Problems
Multi-Step Strategy
Identify ALL steps before solving
Read the entire problem first. Identify how many steps are needed. Plan each step before calculating. Check that each intermediate answer makes sense before continuing.
Worked Example
Each box has 24 items. 3 full boxes + 1 box with 15:
Step 1: 3 × 24 = 72
Step 2: 72 + 15 = 87 items
8. Rate Problems
Unit Rate
rate = quantity ÷ units
A rate compares two different quantities with different units. To find the unit rate, divide the total quantity by the number of units. The unit rate tells you "per one" of something.
Worked Example
Drive 150 miles in 3 hours:
Rate = 150 ÷ 3 = 50 mph
9. Scaling / Ratio
Proportion Formula
a/b = c/d → cross multiply
When two ratios are equal, you can cross multiply to find the unknown value. Set up the proportion with matching units in the same positions. Double number lines help visualize the relationship.
Worked Example
Recipe: 2 cups for 6 servings. For 15 servings:
2/6 = x/15 → 6x = 30 → x = 5 cups
10. Coordinate Graphing
Coordinate Rule
(x, y) → right x, up y
Use ordered pairs (x, y) to describe locations on a grid. Start at the origin (0,0), move right x units along the horizontal axis, then up y units. Graph data points to see patterns.
Worked Example
Point (4, 1): go right 4, up 1
Points (1,1), (2,2), (3,3) form a straight line
11. Fraction Division in Context
Dividing by a Fraction
a ÷ (1/n) = a × n
When you divide by a unit fraction, flip it and multiply. Think: "How many 1/2-foot pieces fit in 4 feet?" Each foot has 2 halves, so 4 feet has 8 halves.
Worked Example
4 feet of ribbon cut into 1/2-ft pieces:
4 ÷ 1/2 = 4 × 2 = 8 pieces
12. Area of Irregular Shapes
Decompose Strategy
A_total = A₁ + A₂
Split irregular shapes into rectangles or triangles you know how to measure. Calculate each part's area separately. Then add all parts together for the total area.
Worked Example
L-shape: top 6×4 = 24, bottom right 3×2 = 6
Total = 24 + 6 = 30 sq units
🔴 Hard
13. Complex Multi-Step
Problem Solving Cycle
Plan → Execute → Check
Complex problems require 3+ operations. Convert mixed numbers to decimals first. Work through operations in order, keeping track of units. Always check your answer makes sense.
Worked Example
5¼ lbs at $1.20/lb, tax $0.35:
5.25 × 1.20 = $6.30
$6.30 + $0.35 = $6.65 total
14. Algebra Setup
Writing Equations
Let x = unknown value
Translate words → math symbols
Identify the unknown and call it x. Translate each phrase: "more than" = +, "twice" = × 2, "is" = =. Then solve for x by working backwards (using inverse operations).
Worked Example
"3 more than twice a number is 15"
2x + 3 = 15 → 2x = 12 → x = 6
15. Pattern Problems
Finding the Rule
Find the rule connecting input to output
Look at how output changes as input increases by 1. If output goes up by the same amount each time, the rule is multiplication. Test your rule on every row to verify it's correct.
Worked Example
In: 1, 2, 3, 4 → Out: 5, 10, 15, 20
Rule: multiply by 5 (Output = Input × 5)
16. Data from Tables
Reading Tables
Read carefully; check units
Tables organize data into rows and columns. Read the header row to understand what each column means. Pay attention to units. Use table data to answer questions by finding, comparing, or calculating values.
Worked Example
Heights: 54, 60, 57 inches
Tallest = 60, Shortest = 54
Difference = 60 − 54 = 6 inches
17. Estimation with Justification
Estimation Strategy
Round to leading digit, then compute
Round each number to its leading (largest place value) digit. Then multiply or add the rounded numbers. Use estimation to check whether an exact answer is reasonable.
Worked Example
Estimate 493 × 52:
≈ 500 × 50 = 25,000
Exact: 25,636 ← close, so answer is reasonable
18. Geometry + Algebra
Geometry + Algebra
P = 2l + 2w; solve for unknown
Substitute what you know into the formula and solve for the unknown side. Use inverse operations (subtraction and division) to isolate the variable on one side of the equation.
Worked Example
P = 36, width = 7. Find length:
2l + 2(7) = 36
2l + 14 = 36 → 2l = 22 → l = 11
