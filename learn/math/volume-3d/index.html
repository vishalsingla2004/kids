🟢 Easy
1. What is Volume?
Definition
Volume = amount of 3D space a shape takes up
Volume measures how much space is inside a 3D object. We count unit cubes — each cube is 1 cm³ (one cubic centimetre).
  • Units: cm³, m³, mm³ (always cubed)
  • 1 litre = 1,000 cm³
  • 1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³
Worked Example
A box that is 2 wide × 3 long × 2 tall holds 2 × 3 × 2 = 12 unit cubes → Volume = 12 cm³
2. Cube
Formula
V = s³ = s × s × s
A cube has all sides equal. Multiply the side length by itself three times.
Worked Example
Side s = 4 cm
V = 4 × 4 × 4 = 4³ = 64 cm³
3. Rectangular Prism (Cuboid)
Formula
V = length × width × height
A rectangular prism (also called a cuboid) is like a box — all faces are rectangles. Multiply all three dimensions together.
Worked Example
l = 5 cm, w = 3 cm, h = 2 cm
V = 5 × 3 × 2 = 30 cm³
4. Cylinder
Formula
V = π × r² × h
The base is a circle (area = πr²). Multiply by the height to get the volume. Use π ≈ 3.14 unless told otherwise.
Worked Example
r = 3 cm, h = 10 cm
V = 3.14 × 3² × 10 = 3.14 × 9 × 10 = 282.6 cm³
5. Units of Volume
Conversions
1 m³ = 1,000,000 cm³
1 litre = 1,000 cm³
1 m³ = 1,000 litres
  • Volume units are always cubed (³)
  • 1 cm³ = 1 millilitre (mL)
  • 1,000 mL = 1 litre (L)
  • 1,000 L = 1 m³
Quick Check
A fish tank of volume 20,000 cm³ holds 20 litres of water.
6. Comparing Volumes
Key Idea
Different shapes can have the same volume
A tall thin box and a short wide box can hold exactly the same amount. More surface area does NOT mean more volume.
  • 2×2×8 = 32 cm³ (tall & narrow)
  • 4×4×2 = 32 cm³ (flat & wide)
  • Always calculate — don't assume by looks!
🟡 Medium
1. Triangular Prism
Formula
V = ½ × b × h × L
(b = base, h = triangle height, L = length)
First find the area of the triangular face (½ × base × height), then multiply by the length of the prism.
Worked Example
b = 4, h = 3, L = 10
V = ½ × 4 × 3 × 10 = 60 cm³
2. Cone
Formula
V = ⅓ × π × r² × h
A cone is exactly one-third of a cylinder with the same base and height. Always remember the ⅓!
Worked Example
r = 3, h = 7
V = ⅓ × 3.14 × 9 × 7 = ⅓ × 197.82 = 65.94 cm³
3. Pyramid (Square Base)
Formula
V = ⅓ × s² × h
(⅓ × base area × height)
Like the cone, a pyramid is one-third of the prism with the same base and height. Base area of a square = s².
Worked Example
s = 6, h = 10
V = ⅓ × 36 × 10 = ⅓ × 360 = 120 cm³
4. Sphere
Formula
V = (4/3) × π × r³
A sphere is a perfect ball. Every point on the surface is the same distance (radius r) from the centre. Cube the radius, multiply by π and 4/3.
Worked Example
r = 3
V = (4/3) × 3.14 × 27 = 4/3 × 84.78 = 113.04 cm³
5. Volume vs Surface Area
Key Distinction
Volume = inside space
Surface Area = total outside area
These measure different things! Volume uses units³ (cm³), surface area uses units² (cm²). Don't mix up the formulas.
  • Volume: how much it holds
  • Surface area: how much wrapping paper you need
  • A bigger surface area does NOT mean bigger volume
6. Liquid Volume
Key Fact
1 cm³ = 1 mL
1,000 cm³ = 1 litre
Containers hold liquids. Volume in cm³ directly converts to mL. This is very useful for fish tanks, bottles and measuring jugs!
Worked Example
Fish tank: 40 cm × 20 cm × 25 cm
V = 40 × 20 × 25 = 20,000 cm³
= 20,000 mL = 20 litres
🔴 Hard
1. Composite 3D Shapes
Method
Total V = V₁ + V₂
(add volumes of each part)
Break a complex shape into simpler parts you know how to calculate. Find each volume separately, then add them.
Worked Example
Cuboid: 10×5×3 = 150 cm³
Cylinder: π×2²×4 = 3.14×4×4 ≈ 50.24 cm³
Total = 150 + 50.24 = 200.24 cm³
2. Hollow Shapes
Hollow Cylinder Formula
V = π(R² − r²) × h
For a hollow shape, subtract the volume of the hole from the total volume. For a pipe: outer cylinder minus inner cylinder.
Worked Example
Outer R = 5, inner r = 3, h = 20
V = π × (25 − 9) × 20 = 3.14 × 16 × 20 = 1,004.8 cm³
3. Rate Problems
Rate Formula
Time = Volume ÷ Rate
First find the total volume. Convert to the same units as the flow rate. Then divide volume by flow rate to get time.
Worked Example
Tank: 2 m × 1.5 m × 1 m = 3 m³
Convert: 3 m³ = 3,000 litres
Fill rate: 30 L/min
Time = 3,000 ÷ 30 = 100 minutes
4. Scaling Volumes
Scale Rule
Scale factor k → Volume scales by k³
Doubling all dimensions (k=2) multiplies volume by 2³ = 8, not 2! Tripling dimensions multiplies volume by 27.
  • k = 2 → volume × 8
  • k = 3 → volume × 27
  • k = 0.5 → volume × 0.125 (halved dims → ⅛ volume)
Example
A box has V = 24 cm³. If all sides double,
new V = 24 × 2³ = 24 × 8 = 192 cm³
5. Cross-Section Method
Universal Prism Formula
V = Cross-section Area × Length
Any shape that has the same cross-section all the way through is a prism. Find the area of the cross-section face, then multiply by the length. This works for L-shapes, T-shapes, any profile!
  • Cuboid: cross-section = rectangle
  • Cylinder: cross-section = circle
  • Triangular prism: cross-section = triangle
6. Multi-Step Problem Solving
Trapezoid Cross-section
Area = ½(a + b) × h
The pool has a trapezoidal cross-section. Find the cross-section area, then multiply by width to get volume.
Worked Example — Swimming Pool
Shallow depth: 1.5 m, deep end: 2.5 m, length: 25 m, width: 10 m
Cross-section area = ½ × (1.5 + 2.5) × 25 = ½ × 4 × 25 = 50 m²
Volume = 50 × 10 = 500 m³
